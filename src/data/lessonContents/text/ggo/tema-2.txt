# Topic 2 · Delivering Value in IT Governance (Entrega de valor en el gobierno de TI)

## Objectives (Objetivos)
- Understand how to define and measure the value generated by IT investments.
- Review ITIL 4 and Val IT principles linked to value delivery (entrega de valor).
- Identify practices that secure benefits throughout the service life cycle (ciclo de vida del servicio).

## What do we mean by value? (¿Qué entendemos por valor?)
- There is no single definition; it depends on the benefits perceived by customers and stakeholders (beneficios percibidos por clientes y stakeholders).
- The IT Governance Institute notes that value materializes when initiatives arrive **on time (a tiempo)**, **within budget (dentro del presupuesto)**, and **with the promised benefits (beneficios prometidos)**.
- ITIL 4 defines value as “perceived benefits, usefulness, and importance” (beneficios, utilidad e importancia percibida).

## ITIL 4 guiding principles (Principios de ITIL 4)
- **Focus on value (Centrarse en el valor):** understand how consumers use services and measure their impact on business goals.
- Embed the value focus in day-to-day operations and improvement projects.
- Empower staff to spot opportunities for continuous value (valor continuo).

## Val IT framework (Marco Val IT)
- Created by ITGI to maximize business value (valor de negocio) from IT investments.
- Structured into two domains:
  - **Governance (Gobierno):** integrates value practices across the organization and secures optimal portfolio value.
  - **Management (Gestión):** ensures each individual investment delivers value by monitoring key metrics.
- Guiding questions: Are we doing the right things (¿estamos haciendo lo correcto)? Are we getting the expected benefits (¿obtenemos los beneficios esperados)?
- Complements COBIT, which focuses on proper process execution.

## Value creation process (Proceso de creación de valor)
1. **Strategic demand (Demandas estratégicas)** produces a conceptual service model—business vision plus architectural attributes.
2. **Logical design (Diseño lógico)** turns that vision into detailed requirements and technical architecture.
3. **Transition (Transición)** readies the service for production, catalog inclusion, and support.
4. **Operation and monitoring (Operación y monitorización)** track metrics, resolve incidents, and protect service levels.

## Measuring and communicating value (Medición y comunicación del valor)
- Key steps from Pratt and Herrmann:
  1. Analyze business objectives and context (objetivos de negocio y contexto).
  2. Identify stakeholders and expectations (stakeholders y expectativas).
  3. Model business capabilities and their relationship with IT (capacidades de negocio y su relación con TI).
  4. Define metrics linking IT outcomes to business benefits (beneficios empresariales).
  5. Craft a narrative and communication plan that explains the impact (plan de comunicación del impacto).

## Good practices (Buenas prácticas)
- Prioritize initiatives by their contribution to strategic goals (objetivos estratégicos).
- Keep the portfolio balanced across innovation, improvement, and operations.
- Review benefits regularly and adjust investments according to results.
- Maintain transparency in costs and risks to support decision-making.
