# Session 3 · Cloud service and deployment models (Modelos de servicio y despliegue en la nube)

## Objectives (Objetivos)
- Differentiate IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS models and how responsibilities are shared (reparto de responsabilidades).
- Analyze scenarios that use public, private, and hybrid clouds (nubes públicas, privadas e híbridas).
- Introduce criteria for choosing providers and controlling costs.

## Cloud computing refresher (Recordatorio de computación en la nube)
- Shared compute, storage, and networking resources available on demand (recursos compartidos bajo demanda).
- Elasticity, self-service (autoservicio), and pay-per-use as defining traits.

## Service models (Modelos de servicio)
- **IaaS:** granular control over VMs, networks, and storage; you manage the OS, runtime, and patches (SO, runtime y parches).
- **PaaS:** the platform runs the runtime; the team focuses on code and data—great for web apps and APIs.
- **SaaS:** complete applications consumed as a service; the customer handles configuration and user management.

## Choosing a model (Selección del modelo)
- Key variables: delivery speed, control requirements, regulatory compliance, team skills (habilidades del equipo).
- Real portfolios mix models depending on each workload’s needs.

## Leading providers (Proveedores principales)
- **AWS:** broad service catalog, leadership in IaaS, and a rich serverless ecosystem.
- **Azure:** tight integration with the Microsoft stack, hybrid capabilities, and enterprise governance tools.
- **Google Cloud:** strong data/ML services, container innovation, and high-performance networking.

## Multi-cloud and hybrid strategies (Estrategias multi-cloud e híbridas)
- Reasons: resilience, data locality, negotiation leverage (resiliencia, localización de datos, negociación).
- Costs: duplicated operations, designing for the lowest common denominator, network and identity complexity.
- Pragmatic approach: aim for application portability and place governed data where it creates the most value.

## Public, private, and hybrid clouds (Nube pública, privada e híbrida)
- **Public (Pública):** shared infrastructure; elasticity and lower CAPEX, with compliance and noisy-neighbor challenges.
- **Private (Privada):** full control and customization; higher investment and risk of overprovisioning.
- **Hybrid (Híbrida):** combines both to respect data gravity and latency; demands disciplined operational coordination.

## Highlight use cases (Casos de uso destacados)
- **Web applications (Aplicaciones web):** stateless tiers on PaaS/serverless, managed databases, blue-green deployments.
- **Big data & analytics:** object storage data lakes, serverless pipelines, catalogs, and governance.
- **AI/ML:** managed notebooks, GPU/TPU training, MLOps pipelines.
- **Backup & disaster recovery (Backup & DR):** cross-region replication, immutable backups, periodic recovery drills.

## Costs and FinOps (Costes y FinOps)
- Pricing models: on-demand, reserved/committed, spot/preemptible, serverless.
- Monitor egress fees and managed-service premiums.
- FinOps practices: tagging, budgets, automated shutdowns, and right-sizing.

## Governance and compliance (Gobierno y cumplimiento)
- Shared responsibility model: the provider secures the cloud; the customer secures what runs on it.
- Design for data residency, audit, and retention requirements (residencia de datos, auditoría y retención).
- Plan exit strategies to mitigate lock-in—portable formats, backups, automation.
